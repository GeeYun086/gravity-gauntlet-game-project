## 🧩 Game Overview

**Gravity Gauntlet** is a first-person physics-based puzzle platformer set in a damaged space station.

Players navigate through interconnected areas by manipulating gravity, such as gravity cubes, to solve puzzles and traverse unstable environments. What begins as controlling the gravity of objects gradually expands into more complex interactions, including player gravity manipulation and multi-step puzzle systems.

The game combines physics, spatial reasoning, and level-based progression to create a continuous learning experience.

<br><br><br>

## 🌌 Design Concept

The core concept is **escaping a damaged space station using gravity as the primary tool**.

The design focuses on:
- First-person puzzle-platforming
- Gravity-based interaction with objects and environment
- Area-based progression with increasing complexity
- Learning through interaction rather than explicit instruction

Inspired by games like *Portal* and *Gravitas*, the project emphasizes mechanic-driven gameplay and structured progression rather than narrative-heavy storytelling.

<br><br><br>

## ⚙️ Core Mechanics & Design

### 🧲 Gravity-Based Interaction
- Change gravity direction to solve puzzles  
- Objects and environment react dynamically  
- Later stages expand mechanics to player gravity  

### 🧩 Level Design Structure
- **Learn → Apply → Expand**
- Mechanics are introduced gradually and recombined in later stages
- Focus on readability, clarity, and intuitive understanding

### 🎯 Player Experience Goals
- Encourage creative problem-solving  
- Deliver clear “aha” moments  
- Maintain consistent and immersive gameplay flow

## 🛠 Development Process

Developed over one semester in the **Game Studio course** at the University of Würzburg through iterative development and weekly team collaboration.

### 📌 Concept
- Defined core idea: gravity-based puzzle platformer  
- Established space station setting and progression concept  

### 🧪 Prototyping
- Built early gravity interaction systems  
- Developed physics-based player controller  
- Explored puzzle ideas and gameplay direction  

### 🧱 Consolidation
- Refactored core systems and stabilized mechanics  
- Expanded gameplay elements (objects, interactions, puzzles)  
- Continued level greyboxing and iteration  

### 🏗 Final Game Assembly
- Integrated levels into a connected structure  
- Combined gameplay, art, UI, and sound systems  
- Implemented progression systems and core features  

### ✨ Polishing
- Bug fixing and optimization  
- UI/UX refinement and feedback adjustments  
- Final testing and release preparation  

### 🔁 Development Approach
- Weekly progress reports and team meetings  
- Playtesting-driven iteration  
- GitLab-based workflow (issues, branches, merge requests)  
- Cross-team collaboration (Design, Programming, Art)
